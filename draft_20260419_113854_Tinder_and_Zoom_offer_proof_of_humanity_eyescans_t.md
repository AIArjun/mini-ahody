**ARTICLE BODY:**  
Tinder and Zoom are adopting advanced eye-scanning technology to help users verify their identities amid growing concerns about artificial intelligence impersonation. This initiative allows users to scan their irises to earn a "proof of humanity" badge on their profiles.

The eye-scanning process can be completed through an online app or a specialized device developed by World, formerly known as Worldcoin. This technology is part of Tools for Humanity, a start-up co-founded by Sam Altman, who also leads OpenAI. Once verified, users receive a unique identification code stored on their smartphones, referred to as their World ID.

The announcement was made during a live event in San Francisco, where Altman emphasized the increasing prevalence of AI-generated content online. He noted that distinguishing between human and AI-generated material is crucial for the future of online interactions.

Both Tinder and Zoom have faced challenges with fake accounts and scams, particularly on Tinder, where users have reported encountering numerous bots. A personal blog post from user Victoria Brooks highlighted that nearly 30% of profiles she encountered were suspected scams, often using AI-generated scripts to manipulate real users.

To enhance user safety, Tinder previously required users to submit video selfies for verification. The integration of World ID is seen as a significant step forward in ensuring that users can trust the authenticity of those they interact with online.
