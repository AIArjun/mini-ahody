**ARTICLE BODY:**  
Ben Roberts-Smith, Australia's most decorated living soldier, has publicly denied all allegations against him following his recent charges of war crimes. The Victoria Cross recipient was charged with five counts of murder last week and released on bail on Friday.

In a statement, the 47-year-old former corporal of the Special Air Service (SAS) expressed pride in his service in Afghanistan and stated he would use the legal proceedings as an opportunity to "finally" clear his name. "I understand this journey will be difficult. But I can promise everybody that I have never run from a fight in my life," he said.

Roberts-Smith is accused of involvement in the deaths of unarmed Afghan detainees between 2009 and 2012. He described the circumstances surrounding his arrest as a "sensational" and "unnecessary spectacle" and declined to answer questions from the media.

The charges come after a 2023 civil defamation case in which a judge found "substantial truth" to some of the murder claims against him. Roberts-Smith now faces one charge of murder, one of jointly commissioning a murder, and three of aiding or abetting murder.

The presiding judge noted the exceptional nature of his case, suggesting that he could face years in custody before a trial if not granted bail.
